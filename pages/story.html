NeverWhere

In the mystical land of Shadowdale, a daring adventure awaits three unlikely companions - Kael, Brocc, and Apollo. Brocc, a youthful gnome and 5th level moon druid, joins forces with the towering mountain barbarian, Kael, and the charismatic human bard, Apollo, who boasts an impressive repertoire of stories and musical talents. Their journey begins when Aumry, a seasoned guide, leads them to meet Malagar, the Arch Mage of Waterdeep, who hires the team to negotiate the release of Pan the Gnome from the clutches of the nefarious Penny Eyesore, who has imprisoned him in the dark and dangerous sewers beneath Shadowdale.

During their mission, the group encounters Rictor the Lucky, a human thief who reveals that their world is not as it seems, and introduces them to the powerful Witch of Shadowdale, Sylune, and the wizard protector, El Minister.

Sylune Silverhand

As they navigate their way through the underground market, the team is able to secure Pan's freedom through shrewd negotiation. Kael even offers up some of his own cherished memories to ensure the safe release of both Pan and Rictor. However, their quest takes an unexpected turn when they discover that events in their world are being rewritten, and their journey leads them to Tyrus the Wall City. There, they find the wooden sword relic that they were tasked with retrieving, but Pan disappears with the sword, leaving the group to wonder about his motives.

Penny Eyesore, who had been trailing the team, comes to their aid during a fierce battle, and leads them north towards her sister, the Ice Queen. It turns out that Pan had delivered the sword to the Ice Queen, but Penny negotiates with her sibling for the relic's safe return and Pan's release. But a tragic mistake ensues when Brocc casts an Entanglement spell on Penny, causing her to inadvertently kill him. Apollo steps in to negotiate with Penny, offering up one of his own cherished memories to bring Brocc back to life.

The group ultimately returns to Malagar, who unsheathes the wooden sword, freeing Sylune, El Minister, and Penny from their illusion. They learn that a sinister wizard named Pyatt has been manipulating time and space in an effort to find his lost love, drawing people from different times and places into their world. Malagar and the team realize that they need to stop Pyatt and restore reality. To do so, they seek out the Secret Seven, a group of powerful allies who may be able to help them. The team needs to attend a gathering where the Secret Seven are expected to appear, and Talim, a fierce female Drow freedom fighter, may hold the key to securing an invitation.

El Minister

With danger lurking at every turn, the trio of Kael, Brocc, and Apollo must summon all their strength and courage to confront the challenges that await them on their perilous journey. Deep within the shadows of the Underdark, this team of adventurers stumbled upon two intriguing figures, Jai Kai, a devout human cleric and RaZaa, a towering Dragonborn who was abruptly whisked away by a higher being. Talim, a battered soldier, crossed paths with the team and informed them of her recent betrayal by Xander, a drow who absconded with six of her troops. In exchange for their help, she promised to provide the team with an invitation.

Tracking Xander to the sewers in the northeast, the team brokered a deal, exchanging Talim's whereabouts for the magical invitation. However, they reneged on their promise and instead cut a deal with Xander, bartering the invitation for Talim's location.

Talim

After delivering the invitation to Malagar, the team intended to take a hiatus, but their respite was short-lived. Summoned by Thurbal, the warden of Shadowdale, they were tasked with reconnoitering the abandoned tunnels beneath the castle. Their exploration encountered several obstacles, particularly the challenging Dark Elves, but the rumors of Talim's treachery spread like wildfire throughout the Underdark.

During their perilous journey, the team stumbled upon a letter addressed to Talim on a dead Drow, detailing a faction that opposed the enslavement of others and hoped to unify the people. The team battled various foes, with Apollo's stealth and Brocc's markings on the wall aiding their progress. They even encountered undead ogres, with one wearing a vial of glowing liquid around his neck. Brocc transformed into a spider and snatched the vial from the ogre, hoping to use it against their adversaries. After throwing it at the remaining ogre, they discovered that it contained Penny Eyesore's blood, causing the ogre to grow into a towering monster. Dubbing himself as the Scion of Penny, the creature proved too powerful for the team until Brocc's thunderclap spell and Jai Kai's Eldith blast caused the ceiling to cave in on top of it. As they fled the scene, they stumbled upon Simon The Stone Breaker, a dwarf whose team had been decimated by the same glowing creature. The team spent several days in the Underdark, charting their progress and honing their skills in preparation for the next challenge that awaited them.

Malagar

For many long and grueling days, the team braved the dangers of the Under Dark, battling fearsome creatures and navigating the treacherous terrain. But in the end, it was one of their own who nearly spelled their doom. Zeek, one of Talim's so-called "children," had joined the team under false pretenses, masquerading as a guide and leading them into a deadly trap. Though they managed to survive, it came at a great cost, with each adventurer suffering serious injuries and one, Jai Kai, losing his life to the swarming enemies that dragged him away to be devoured. The team was able to make their way back to the surface, only to discover that they were not the only ones to have encountered a great danger in the Under Dark.

Thurbal and his army met them at the exit, the entrance having been blasted wide open by a Green Glowing Monster. The creature had destroyed half of Thurbal's army before disappearing into the wilderness, referring to itself as The Scion of Penny. The team stayed silent about their own role in unleashing the monster, but their cover was soon blown by a Drow who recognized them and informed Thurbal of their involvement.

Xander

Fearing persecution, the team was forced to flee to Shadyvale, a town on a different plane of existence where magic was taboo. They sought the help of Abel, a skilled forger, only to discover that he had died. Instead, they set their sights on his brother Babel, who was locked up in jail in Carbone. The team negotiated a deal with the Van Cross Family for his release, but the terms were steep: they had 24 hours to locate and deliver the body of Wilham, the Van Cross child, who had been murdered. Brocc managed to revive Wilham long enough to reveal that Katherine Chambers was guilty of his death, leading the Van Cross to order her execution. But when Katherine's spirit escaped and it was discovered that she was innocent, the team found themselves at odds with their former allies.

With the forgery in hand, the team met with Malagar, who instructed them to travel to Owl Ridge and hire a boat to take them to Ibrido, where the Secret Seven would be meeting. Their journey would take them through the Elder Forest, where they would face new dangers and challenges. And so, with heavy hearts and determined spirits, the team forged ahead, ready to face whatever lay ahead.
